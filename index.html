Task A — Individual Presentation
📖
What this page is for
Study the PREP structure, read the annotated model answer, and understand what examiners look for. This is a guided walkthrough — not a timed test. Go to Task A Test when ready.
PREP Framework — Official Task A Structure (MPM MUET SOP)
Every individual presentation must follow this order
P
Point
State your assigned position directly in the opening sentence
R
Reason
Explain WHY your point is valid or beneficial
E
Example
Support with ONE specific Malaysian example or statistic
P
Restate
Close by reinforcing your main position confidently
How to use PREP in this test
1
Start with your exact point
Say your assigned idea clearly. Do not begin too generally for too long.
2
Explain one strong reason
Tell the examiner why your point matters. One clear reason is better than many weak reasons.
3
Give one Malaysian example
Use a local situation, programme, student experience, or familiar issue to make your answer more convincing.
4
End by repeating your stand
Finish confidently so the examiner hears a complete answer, not an unfinished idea.

What the examiner assesses (§20 Production)
1
Task Fulfilment
Did the candidate address their specific assigned point? Did they complete PREP structure?
2
Fluency & Coherence
Smooth delivery? Ideas logically connected with discourse markers?
3
Vocabulary Range
Varied and appropriate vocabulary beyond basic words?
4
Language Accuracy
Grammar generally correct? Errors minor (Band 4+) or impeding communication (Band 2–3)?
5
Communicative Ability
Engages audience? Appropriate register? Clear opening and closing?
6
Pronunciation
Clear enough to be understood? Accent is acceptable; clarity is what is assessed.

Band improvement guide
1–2
Complete one full PREP sentence
State your point + one reason. Two accurate sentences beats five confused ones.
3
Add a specific Malaysian example
"For example, in Malaysia..." moves from Band 3.0 to 3.5.
4
Use 3+ discourse markers
"Furthermore", "This is mainly because", "As a result" push to Band 4.0+.
5
Upgrade vocabulary + integrate sub-themes
"good" → "beneficial"; develop your point with additional supporting ideas for Band 5.

🔗
Same topic as Task A — this is the official MUET SOP requirement
In the real exam, the group does NOT change topic for Task B. They continue with the same situation from Task A and must reach consensus on ONE best point.
Model Group Discussion — Career Ready topic
Annotated transcript showing Band 4.5 interaction behaviours
Model interaction transcript (10 min group discussion)
Candidate A: I would like to begin our discussion. I think mandatory internship programmes is the most effective way to improve graduate employability because students gain real workplace experience that cannot be replicated in classrooms.
Candidate B: That is a valid point, Candidate A. AGREES + BUILDS I would also add that internships help students build professional networks. However, I believe reforming the university curriculum would have a longer-lasting impact as it affects all students.
Candidate C: I understand your point, Candidate B. POLITE DISAGREE However, curriculum reform takes many years. Malaysia still has thousands of unemployed graduates right now. INVITES Candidate D, what is your view on this?
Candidate D: Thank you for asking. I support employer-academia partnerships because they address both issues simultaneously — companies help redesign curricula AND offer placements. This is already working in Germany's dual education model.
Candidate A: That is an excellent point. CONSENSUS Shall we agree that employer-academia partnerships is the most effective solution since it combines curriculum improvement with practical experience?
Candidate B: Yes, I agree. Employer-academia partnerships address the problem from both sides. I support this as our group's answer.
What made this Band 4.5?

Each candidate responded to a specific person by name
"Candidate A, that is a valid point..." — shows active listening, not just waiting to speak.
âœ“
Both agreement AND polite disagreement used
Not just agreeing — Candidate B disagreed with a reason, showing independent thinking.
âœ“
A question was asked to invite a quieter candidate
"Candidate D, what is your view?" — inviting others raises Interaction score significantly.
âœ“
Group reached clear consensus before time ended
"Shall we agree that..." is a Band 4+ consensus move. Missing this reduces Task Fulfilment.
Interaction phrase bank
Memorise these before your test
Agreeing
I completely agree with Candidate A because...
That is a valid point. I would also add that...
Building on what Candidate B said, I think...
Disagreeing politely
I see your point, however I believe...
With respect, I have a slightly different view...
That may be true, but we should also consider...
Asking / Inviting
Candidate C, what are your thoughts on this?
Would anyone like to add to this point?
Shall we agree that [point] is the best solution?
Can you elaborate on that further?
Task B examiner criteria (§20 Interaction)
1
Task Fulfilment (Task B)
Did the group reach consensus? Did the student contribute meaningfully to the decision?
2
Interaction Quality
Responding to others, agreeing/disagreeing with reasons, inviting quieter members.
3
Vocabulary in Context
Using appropriate discussion language beyond basic phrases.
4
Language Accuracy
Grammar accurate enough for communication to succeed.
5
Communicative Ability
Taking initiative, maintaining flow, managing turn-taking.
6
Interaction (dedicated criterion)
Responding to others, building on their points, moving discussion forward.
📌 §20 Band 5.0 Interaction: "Can formulate ideas and opinions with precision and relate their contribution skillfully to those of other speakers."
